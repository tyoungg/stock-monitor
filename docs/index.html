Owner Earnings: Net Income + Stock-Based Compensation (SBC) − Buybacks − RSU Tax.
Owner Yield: Owner Earnings / Market Cap — baseline valuation.
Real Yield: (Owner Earnings + max(0, Buybacks − SBC)) / Market Cap — true shareholder return after offsetting dilution.

SBC %: SBC / Net Income — portion of earnings paid in equity.
Buyback Quality: SBC / Buybacks — % of buybacks used to offset dilution (lower is better).

Dilution (3Y): CAGR of share count — measures ownership erosion.
3Y Per-Share Growth: Revenue CAGR − Dilution — growth adjusted for dilution.
🟢 Strong: High owner earnings, low SBC, and buybacks primarily return capital.
🟡 Mixed: Moderate earnings quality or offsetting factors.
🔴 Distorted: Low owner earnings, high SBC, or buybacks mostly offset dilution.
